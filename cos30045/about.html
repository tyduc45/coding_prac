About This Project

The Appliance Energy Consumption Website was developed as part of the COS30045 Web Data Visualisation unit at Swinburne University of Technology. The project aims to present household appliance energy data in an accessible, visually engaging format to help Australian consumers make informed purchasing and usage decisions.

Australia has some of the highest residential electricity prices in the world. By presenting clear, data-driven information about energy consumption, this site empowers households to reduce their electricity bills and lower their environmental footprint.

Our Mission

💡

Educate

Provide clear, accurate information about how different appliances consume energy and what the real-world cost implications are for Australian households.

📊

Visualise

Present energy data in intuitive formats — tables, charts, and comparisons — so that complex information is easy to understand at a glance.

🌱

Empower

Give households the knowledge to choose more efficient appliances and adopt behaviours that reduce both energy costs and carbon emissions.

Data Sources & References

The energy consumption data and statistics referenced across this website are drawn from the following authoritative Australian sources:

  • Australian Government Energy Rating — energyrating.gov.au
    Product energy ratings, running cost calculators, and consumer guides.
  • Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)
    National household energy use statistics and policy frameworks.
  • Australian Energy Regulator (AER) — aer.gov.au
    Retail electricity tariff data and market reports.
  • Your Home — Australia's Guide to Environmentally Sustainable Homes
    yourhome.gov.au — practical guidance for reducing home energy use.

Disclaimer

This website was created for educational purposes as part of a university assignment. All energy figures presented are indicative estimates based on publicly available data and are intended to illustrate general trends rather than provide exact energy auditing advice. Actual consumption will vary based on usage habits, appliance settings, and individual product models.

For personalised energy advice, consult your state or territory energy efficiency authority or a qualified energy auditor.
